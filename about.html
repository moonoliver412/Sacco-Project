A community cooperative with purpose.

For a decade, Jiunge Upate SACCO Society has been quietly building financial confidence in PCEA Dandora — one shared deposit, one fair loan, one disciplined member at a time.

2015
Year Established

PCEA Dandora SACCO Society Limited (J.U.S.S Ltd) is a Christian-based savings and credit cooperative society — established by members of PCEA Dandora Church who believed that financial empowerment belongs in the hands of community.

We are registered under the Cooperative Societies Act No. 12 of 1997 and governed by the Cooperative Societies Act of 2008 and our own bylaws — providing a regulated, transparent, faith-aligned home for members' savings and a fair source of credit when life calls for it.

Our work is straightforward: we improve the economic and social well-being of our members and the wider community we belong to. We do this through disciplined savings products, accessible loans, and education that helps members make confident financial decisions.

— 01 / Direction

Where we're going, why it matters.

Two short statements that anchor every product, every meeting, every member conversation we have.

Our Vision

To remain a model SACCO empowering the community economically, socially, and spiritually.

We aim to be the cooperative other communities look to — not for size, but for the integrity of how we serve the people who entrust us with their savings.

Our Mission

To promote a saving and investment culture for sustainable economic and social enhancement of our members and stakeholders.

Practical, daily, member-by-member. We help people save more than they thought they could, and borrow only what truly serves them.

— 02 / Values

Nine principles we hold close.

These aren't decoration on a wall. They're the standard we measure every decision against — from the boardroom to the front desk.

i.

Integrity

We do what we say. Members' trust is the only currency that compounds without limit.

ii.

Teamwork

A SACCO is, by definition, plural. We rise on the strength of one another.

iii.

Accountability

Every shilling has a name. Every decision has a record.

iv.

Commitment

To members, to the long view, and to the cooperative model itself.

v.

Transparency

Books open, rules clear, fees disclosed before they're due.

vi.

Excellence in Service

The standard of care we'd want for our own family — because often, members are.

vii.

Inclusivity

viii.

ix.

Stewardship

Resources entrusted to us are never ours alone. We hold them in trust.
